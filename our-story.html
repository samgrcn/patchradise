The Beginning

How It All Started

From raw necks and ruined sessions to a product that changed the way surfers suit up.

🧮
From surgical dressings to surf patches: a material science breakthrough
🌊 California Coast

A daily frustration became a mission

It started with a group of daily surfers on the California coast who were fed up. Every session left them with raw, irritated skin that burned for hours after they left the water. They tried everything they could find.

Petroleum jelly rubbed off within minutes. Rash guards under wetsuits added uncomfortable bulk. Body Glide needed constant reapplication. Talc powder was a mess and did nothing once wet. Nothing worked for more than a few waves.

"We realized nobody was solving this because nobody understood both the ocean and the science. We happened to know both."

One of the founders had a background in medical materials engineering. The breakthrough came when they realized that surgical wound dressings were designed for exactly this: adhesive barriers that stay put on wet skin, breathe, and peel off painlessly. They just needed to be reimagined for the surf.

The first prototypes were cut by hand and tested at dawn patrol. Over months of iteration, testing in everything from mushy two-footers to overhead point breaks, the patches evolved. Patchradise was officially born when friends in the lineup started asking for patches before every single session.

The Logic

Why Patches, Not Cream?

We tried every alternative before building Patchradise. Here is why patches win.

💧

Creams wash off in minutes

Barrier creams and petroleum jelly dissolve in saltwater almost immediately. By the time you paddle out and catch your first wave, the protection is already gone. You are back to bare skin against neoprene.

👕

Rash guards add bulk and restrict movement

Wearing a rash guard under your wetsuit sounds logical, but the added layer creates bunching, restricts your paddle stroke, and can actually create new friction points. It trades one problem for another.

🔄

Body Glide needs constant reapplication

Anti-chafe balms work for runners and cyclists, but the ocean is a different story. Saltwater and constant movement break down these products quickly, requiring reapplication that is impossible mid-session.

🎯

Patches deliver 8 hours of targeted protection

A Patchradise patch stays exactly where you put it for up to 8 hours, even in heavy surf. It protects the exact friction zone with a medical-grade barrier, no reapplication needed. One and done.

🌱

No chemicals absorbing into your skin

Unlike creams that rely on chemical ingredients soaking into your skin, patches create a physical barrier. Nothing absorbs. Nothing leaches into the water. Just clean, mechanical protection.

🧮

The result: targeted, long-lasting, chemical-free protection that works as hard as you surf. That is why we built patches, and that is why surfers keep coming back.
